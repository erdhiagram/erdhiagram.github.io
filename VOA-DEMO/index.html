VMware Optimization Assessment provides a step guide to optimize your environment. It gives 360 degree visibility to their capacity, performance andconfiguartion across multi-cloud environments throuh predictive analytics and unified single console.

Optimizing a VMware-based IaaS begins with the end in mind. From your customers viewpoint, the underlying architecture (e.g. SDDC, EUC) is transparent. They buy service, which comes with the 3 sets of SLA.
Each SLA is measured at per VM level. Each VM has its own SLA, independent of other VMs. The SLA is not measured at infrastructure level.​

Availability SLA
This measures the availability of the Guest OS, not just the VM. Just because the underlying virtual machine is powered on, does not mean the Guest OS is up and running.
Performance SLA
Performance SLA is much harder to operationalize as IaaS service provider tends to operate on best effort, and performance is measured by complaint. vRealize Operations come with a set of predefined SLA for each element of IaaS (CPU, memory, disk, network).
Compliance SLA
This measure the availability of the Guest OS, not just VM, just because the underlying virtual machine is powered on , does not mean the Guest OS is up and running.
To deliver the SLA, it’s paramount to understand the dependency among the components. For example, Contention and Utilization are 2 non-overlapping family of metrics. They complement each other, providing a complete set for performance management.

Optimized configuration is important as it impacts performance and capacity. Consistent configuration also reduces complexity of operations. Follow these steps, to guide you focus from the most critical to the least critical.​
The settings are split into 2 (VM and Infrastructure). Focus on the consumer of the IaaS first.​

Avoid these . They directly impact visibility, performance, capacity, or security​
The settings are correct, but they are based on older versions, which may no longer be supported, or incompatible with the current environment​
The settings are correct and up to date. But they are exception, and having amany of them increase complexity of operations.​

Performance management is easiest done by separating the layers that can impact VM or application performance. The reason is different team are responsible. The types of metrics can be grouped into 2 kinds of metrics (contention vs utilization).​

In Capacity Management, you want to maximise the utilization without compromising performance. The higher the utilization, all else being equal, the more productive the infrastructure is doing.​​
Capacity measurement needs enough data to be accurate. For cluster, generally 1 month of data is required so monthly peaks are not missed.​

  • There are 4 things you should do to optimize the Demand part:​
    1. Adjust undersized VM. This corrects the demand and reveals the true demand.​
    2. Adjust oversized VM. This frees up capacity.​
    3. Know the heavy hitters and their peak demands.​
    4. Reclaim wastage, freeing up capacity.
Capacity is optimized when you maximize utilization without compromising performance. You’ve optimized performance, time to look at utilization.​ There are 3 parts of your IaaS capacity:
Compute | Storage | Network.​
